Funerals

The funeral of a loved one, or the scattering of their ashes is a very emotional event. It is your chance to remember them, to say farewell and to pay your respects to the life they lived.

You may want to do this without the intervention of the clergy or the inclusion of a specific religious doctrine. It may be more important to you to capture the personality and beliefs of the person you have lost, to acknowledge and honour the life they lived.

I will support you at this time, and help you to put together a life centred personal ceremony, that celebrates the individual. A ceremony that captures the essence of them; their character and personality. I will work with you to ensure authenticity in everything that is said.

Contact me directly or give my details to your funeral director, and I will meet with you to begin the process. Together, we can plan a funeral tribute or a memorial to meet your requirements.

I am a member of the Institute of Civil Funerals and have recently been awarded their certificate for ‘Going the extra mile for families’.



Life's journey

Life’s journey is like a story; it has a beginning, middle and an end. Love is like a thread running through all the special events during our personal journey. It may be the delightful love of welcoming and naming a new baby. It may be the loyal, faithful love of two people joining in marriage or civil partnership. It may be the sad love of saying farewell at the end of life. At every stage of our life’s journey we are changed and our lives are re-shaped forever. Celebrancy can help you mark these special occasions with love and in a way that captures the very personal nature of your own story.
